## ⚡ Biggest News

1. **OpenAI Unveils Its First Custom Chip Built by Broadcom**
   - OpenAI has introduced its first custom chip, Jalapeño, developed with Broadcom. The ASIC is designed to optimize AI inference processes, enhancing performance for its large language models like GPT. This development marks a significant shift in AI hardware, potentially influencing how AI systems are architected in terms of efficiency and cost.
   
2. **Anthropic Accuses Alibaba of Illicit Claude AI Model Extraction**
   - Anthropic has claimed that Alibaba engaged in unauthorized extraction of capabilities from its Claude AI model. The allegation, if substantiated, could have sweeping implications for AI IP protection and international tech relations, particularly concerning AI ethics and competitive practices.

3. **Patronus AI Lands $50M to Build ‘Digital Worlds’ for AI Agents**
   - Patronus AI, a company focused on stress-testing AI agents through simulated digital environments, has secured $50 million in funding. This indicates rising interest in AI agent resilience and reliability, especially in safety-critical applications like autonomous systems.

4. **OpenAI to Delay GPT-5.6 Release Over Safety Concerns Requested by US Government**
   - The Trump administration has requested OpenAI to slow the release of GPT-5.6 to ensure safety standards, limiting its initial deployment to select partners. This move underscores the growing regulatory scrutiny over AI technologies and their societal impacts.

5. **General Intuition’s $2.3B Investment in Training AI Through Video Games**
   - By investing heavily in using video games to develop AI similar to human intuition, General Intuition explores novel training pathways for AI models. This could redefine standard practices in AI training, bridging gaps between virtual modeling and real-world application.
